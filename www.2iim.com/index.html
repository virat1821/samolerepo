Can a weak student crack cat?
Of course! Students preparing for CAT fall into this myth of CAT is an exam only geniuses can crack. That is not true. The only way for anyone to do well in CAT is consistent and planned preparation. It is as simple as that. The only difference when it comes to a weak student is that they might have to put in lot more effort than the others. That is very much possible if you start your preparation today and study for 3 hours every day. Read this blog article to know more about this - Can a weak student crack CAT Exam?
How many CAT Attempts are allowed?
CAT does not have a limit on the number of attempts or age. You can write CAT exam as many times as you want. But when it comes to getting an admission into IIMs, having 2 to 4 years of work experience is the sweet spot and the maximum number of attempts you can take for being in that sweet spot is 5 attempts.
Can CAT be cleared without coaching?
Every year there are a bunch of students who clear CAT without any coaching. If you are naturally good at Math and English, then it is definitely possible to do that. If you are not one of those people, then taking CAT Coaching makes more sense as it helps you structure your preparation and gives mentorship to help you clear this exam as well as for your Interview Preparation. You can either choose CAT Online coaching or Classroom CAT coaching depending on your preference.
Is Youtube enough for CAT preparation?
Youtube is one of the best resources for free cat preparation. You will get tons of questions for practice and live sessions to learn certain topics. Youtube can be a very good supplementary resource, but it is not enough for CAT preparation.

How much percentile is required to get into IIMs?
Getting calls from IIMs is one thing but getting admissions in IIMs is another thing. This depends on variety of factors starting from your category, gender, your academic performance in 10th,12th and UG, Work Experience, your performance in interviews etc. Generally, you will be required to get 99.5+%ile to get calls from IIM A,B, C and around 95%ile to get calls from new IIMs.

You state repeatedly that the Online CAT Coaching Course has been designed to help one learn at one's own pace. How so?
The online CAT Coaching Course at 2IIM has been constructed as a mixture of slide-decks, videos and quizzes. An average class that is pegged to be covered in, say, around 2.5 hours for the above-average student will comprise of ~80 slides interspersed with around 8-10 videos. The videos are all of 4-10 minutes length and each video focuses on a separate idea. So, if a student is comfortable with 5 of the 8 ideas discussed in the topic, he/she can skip these videos. Student can get to each subsequent slide by pressing "Next" and the speed is entirely governed by the student.

Apart from this, there are on an average two checkpoint quizzes per topic, each with 6-8 questions inside the CAT 2024 - Online CAT preparation. These are simple practice questions to help consolidate the idea. For these also, detailed slide-deck presentations are available, but the student has the option of skipping these if the student is comfortable with the question.

Beyond this, there is a timed Prep-Edge quiz that gives questions at the level of difficulty seen in CAT. These also come with detailed slide-deck solutions. Some of the trickier ones have video solutions as well. Student can choose to skip the solution, rush through the slides, or listen to the videos.

Since we give so much choice over picking the medium for the student, the flexibility over speed is established. A beginner can go through all videos, all solutions in detail, both checkpoint quizzes at his/her own speed, and the prep edge questions after doing some more practice. If done this way, the time taken would be around 5 hours for the class in Online CAT preparation 2024.

Someone who has taken CAT before and knows most of the basics can rush through the slides, listen in on the 4 most critical videos, speed through the checkpoint quizzes and focus on taking the Prep Edge quizzes with utmost seriousness. This 'expert' can finish the topic in less than 75 minutes.

The flexibility over speed is particularly useful while revisiting a topic. You can directly zone in on the 2-3 topics and 4-5 questions that you want to revisit, see 3 key videos and be done with the topic. Some of our students have been absolutely thrilled with the pace at which one can revise with 2IIM's online CAT coaching.

What is the eligibility for CAT exam?
To be eligible for CAT exam, there are a bunch of requirements. Eligibility criteria for admission through CAT may be different and specific to the institute.

1. A CAT exam aspirant with Under Graduation (Correspondence will also do) with minimum 50% marks in aggregate or equivalent from a recognised university.
2. The CAT exam aspirant must have completed education upto Graduation.
3. Candidates in their final year of Graduation are also eligible to apply. A student who finishes diploma and then pursues UG is also eligible.
What is CAT Syllabus ?
CAT Exam is a 2-hour (120 min), computer-based exam. It is not held in offline mode.

The exam comprises of 3 sections— Verbal Ability and Reading Comprehension (VARC), Data Interpretation and Logical Reasoning (DI/LR) and Quantitative Ability (QA).
